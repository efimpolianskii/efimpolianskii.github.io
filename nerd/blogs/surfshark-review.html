Encryption

Surfshark uses AES-256-GCM encryption, which is the strongest encryption available for VPNs. This ensures that your internet traffic is encrypted and protected from eavesdropping.

SurfShark - Encryption Explanation (source)

AES-256-GCM encryption is a type of encryption that is widely considered to be the strongest and most secure form of encryption available for VPNs. Data is encrypted using the Advanced Encryption Standard (AES), which has a 256-bit key length. As there are 2^256 key combinations allowed, it is practically hard to brute force or crack the encryption.

GCM (Galois/Counter Mode) is a block cipher mode of operation that combines AES encryption with a message authentication code (MAC). This provides both confidentiality and integrity to the encrypted data. The MAC ensures that the encrypted data has not been tampered with or modified during transmission, while the encryption ensures that the data is protected from unauthorized access.

High-speed encryption, which is vital for VPNs since it reduces the impact on your internet connection speed, is one of the benefits of AES-256-GCM encryption. Moreover, GCM mode offers a reliable method to authenticate the encrypted data, lowering the possibility of security flaws.
